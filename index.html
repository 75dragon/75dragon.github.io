Fire Emblem Heros!

OwlHacks! My first hackathon at Foothill and an attempt to see how well I can replicate a complicated phone game.
Right before owlhacks commenced, a phone game called Fire Emblem Heros took me and a couple friends by storm.
It was one of those collect units, train them, and send them into 4v4 battles on a tiled world, using stratigy and advantages
to defeat the opposing team. However, they lacked a true PvP aspect (It was all vs CPU) and I wanted to solve this by re-creating
the game from scratch.

I chose Java (because I was the most familiar with it) and decided to work by myself on the project.
The first day was remembering how to structure the basics of the project - I ended up having a
grid of buttons and changed them when charectors moved around the map. Next, we needed to add heros into
the game and make them able to move across the map. After clicking on a tile with a hero, the 2ct true/false would
tell you if the hero could access the tile or not. Then just press on the appropite button, and attack anything in
your weapon's range (1-2 tiles away) and thats the end of your turn!

After everyone was able to move and attack (and deal 0 damage), The map was the next obstical to work on. In the origional game
there are preset maps, probably to add balance and make sure that none would give an unfair advantage. No smart coder want to do
all those preset maps, So I decided to randomly generate them. Lite green are plains, dark green are forests and Grey represents mountains.
this complicates movement, as players are divided into three main catagories and have different movement points on each type of terrain

Unit Type Plains forest Mountain
Infantry 1 2 N/A
Mounted 1 N/A N/A
Flying 1 1 1

well, thats cool and all and a lot of progress. But the whole game (and most others) revolve
around combat with all the other heros. And doing 0 damage will not suffice. After splitting the turn
into 3 steps (hero selection, movement, and attacking) I coded some of the numbers needed for the game.
sadly, combat is still mostly hard coded and defenitly a part I need to improve

I came back to the game near the end of the summer, and wanted to clean up the game (those true false are ugly and
no one would no what they are for). So, to clarify the game, I decided to add tints to indicate where you could move
just like the origional - blue for moment, red for range

With just this I was quite satisfied with the results - one day I might pick up the game again, there is still a lot to work
on such as adding more charectors, re-vamping the combat system (the code looks like a hack!) and making teams.
